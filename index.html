Into the Market

The Silent Rise of Queer Entrepreneurs in Hyderabad

Kiran Raj Gollapalli
Sparsh Organic Group & Law Firm
Core Insight
Highlighted the severe impossibility of securing formal commercial loans for transgender individuals. Utilizes soap manufacturing as economic advocacy, while also practicing law.
Quote
"People from our community rarely get the chance to start a business of their own. Most end up in government jobs. Doing this, building something of our own, feels very new, very difficult."

Sayantan Datta
Independent Journalist (they/them)
Core Insight
Deeply-reported coverage of queer experiences in corporate spaces. Identifies the severe gap between DEI messaging and actual implementation, as well as the rollback of queer-inclusive policies following political shifts in the US.
Quote
"Trump signed those executive orders killing federal DEI programs, which basically shut down diversity councils and cut off a lot of funding. And then what happened? Nearly 37 major companies—IBM, Google, Deloitte—started rolling back their own queer and trans policies. Events got cancelled, hiring initiatives stopped. It's a real domino effect."
Location: Interviewed online.
Vyjayanti Vasanta Mogli
Pro Bono Member, Telangana State Welfare Board for Transgender Persons
Core Insight
Emphasizes the extreme discrimination faced by trans and queer individuals in rural areas and advocates for fundamental legal and economic welfare.
Quote
"I filed RTI to find out how these welfare bodies are even functioning... But even then, most people do not get any benefits. Even if transwomen get benefits from various schemes, some even for women, but what about trans men?"
Location: Interviewed at Lamakaan.
A. Rajendar

Location: Interviewed at the Department office, Nalgonda X Roads, Malakpet.
